South Dakota doesn’t make solar easy. This does.

No state solar office, no mandated net metering, and no single permitting path — every county, city, and utility does it differently. I built a 20kW / 50kWh system on my own property near Lennox and had to piece the process together myself. This is the resource I wish existed.

Why this site exists

I built my own system and couldn’t find a single guide for South Dakota

  • Every other guide assumed the wrong state

    Most solar guides online assume net metering, a state permitting office, and rules that simply don’t exist in South Dakota.

  • Three processes, three timelines

    The SD Electrical Commission handles your wiring permit, your county or city handles the building side, and your utility handles interconnection — separately.

  • Built from a real install

    20kW of solar, 50kWh of battery storage, and 24kW of inverter capacity, self-installed on my own property in Lennox under the homeowner’s exemption.

  • Not a substitute for the professionals

    Nothing here is legal or engineering advice — it’s the plain-language map I had to build by trial and error, so the next person doesn’t start from zero.
