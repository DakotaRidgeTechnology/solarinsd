This does.

South Dakota has no state solar office, no mandated net metering, and no single permitting path — every county, city, and utility does it differently. I built a 20kW / 50kWh system on my own property near Lennox and had to piece the process together myself. This is the resource I wish existed.

01 / NO NET METERING

SD is one of three states with no mandated net metering. What you get paid for excess power is up to your utility, not a state formula.

02 / HOMEOWNER EXEMPTION

SD law lets you pull your own electrical wiring permit and do the work yourself, if you own and live on the property.

03 / LOCAL + UTILITY, SEPARATELY

Your county or city permit and your utility's interconnection agreement are two entirely separate processes, on separate timelines.

Why this site exists

I built my own system and couldn't find a single guide for South Dakota

I put in a ground-up solar and battery system on my own property in Lennox — about 20kW of solar, 50kWh of battery storage, and 24kW of inverter capacity. Every other state-specific guide I found online assumed net metering, assumed a state permitting office, and assumed rules that simply don't apply here.

South Dakota does things differently. There's no statewide net metering mandate, no single building code, and no one-stop solar office. Getting it right means dealing with the South Dakota Electrical Commission for your wiring permit, your own county or city for the building side, and your utility directly for the interconnection agreement — three different processes, three different sets of paperwork, three different timelines.

Nothing on this site is legal or engineering advice, and it isn't a substitute for talking to your inspector, your utility, or a licensed contractor. It's the plain-language map of the process that I had to build by trial and error, written so the next person doesn't have to start from zero.
