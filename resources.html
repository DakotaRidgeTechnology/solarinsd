Forms & Resources

Skip the search results. Here’s where the actual forms and agencies live.

State & utility

  • SD Public Utilities Commission — Solar Energy FAQ
    The PUC’s own plain-language explainer on solar rules, net metering policy, and buyback pricing in South Dakota.
    puc.sd.gov/Publications/solarfaq.aspx
  • SD PUC — Filed generator buyback rates
    Published rates utilities pay for power from small generators under 100kW, filed with and approved by the PUC.
    puc.sd.gov/energy/rrrates.aspx
  • SD Electrical Commission — Homeowner Wiring
    The official page for the homeowner’s exemption and Homeowner’s Wiring Permit application.
    dlr.sd.gov/electrical/homeowner_wiring.aspx
  • SD Electrical Commission — Inspection fee schedule
    Current inspection fees, used alongside the flat $20 permit fee to calculate what you’ll owe.
    dlr.sd.gov/electrical/fees.aspx
  • Xcel Energy — How to Interconnect (South Dakota)
    Xcel’s interconnection process, tariffs, and the Solar Application Portal for customers in their SD territory.
    xcelenergy.com/working_with_us/how_to_interconnect
  • SD Codified Laws
    The full text of state statutes, including SDCL 36-16-15 (homeowner wiring exemption), SDCL 49-34A-42 (exclusive utility territories), and SDCL 10-4-42 to 45 (property tax exemption).
    sdlegislature.gov/Statutes

Federal & general

  • DOE Homeowner’s Guide to Going Solar
    Federal Department of Energy’s general guide, useful for the parts of the process that aren’t state-specific.
    energy.gov/eere/solar/homeowners-guide-going-solar
  • NREL PVWatts Calculator
    Estimate expected production for a given system size and address anywhere in South Dakota.
    pvwatts.nrel.gov

Local — call before you design

Building permits are issued locally, and there’s no statewide portal that covers every jurisdiction. Contact your county’s planning & zoning or building department directly — for Lincoln County and the Sioux Falls metro, for example, that means going through the county or city office rather than a state agency. If you’re inside Sioux Falls or Rapid City city limits, remember your wiring permit is also local, not the state Electrical Commission — see the permits page for that distinction.

Links go to official third-party sites. This guide doesn’t control their content and can’t guarantee it’s current — always confirm against the source directly.
